Fresh Fruit

OFlorida is on track to have the smallest orange crop in almost eight decades. According to the USDA Crop Production Report published October 12, the United States all orange forecast for the 2022-2023 season is 3.19 million tons, down 8 percent from the 2021- 2022 season. The orange forecast, at 28.0 million boxes (1.26 million tons), is down 32 percent. The early, midseason, and Navel varieties are forecast at 11.0 million boxes (495,000 tons), down 40 percent from last season’s final utilization. The Florida Valencia orange forecast, at 17.0 million boxes (765,000 tons), is down 25 percent from last season and is the lowest harvest recorded since 1943.
